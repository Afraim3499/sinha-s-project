In the fast-paced world of international apparel, a good sourcing agent is your greatest asset, and a bad one is your biggest liability. Before you entrust a partner with your production, you must look beyond their website and ask these ten critical questions.

### The Transparency & Ethics Audit

**1. How is your compensation structured?**
Are you operating on an agreed-upon service fee or a project-based model? Transparency here is vital to ensure long-term alignment of interests.

**2. Can you provide audit reports for your active factories?**
Don't just take their word for it. Ask to see recent BSCI, SEDEX, or Accord reports for the factories they actually use.

**3. Do you have your own in-house QC team?**
A professional agent doesn't rely on the factory's own Quality Control. They should have their own inspectors on the ground at every production stage.

### The Operational Capability Check

**4. What is your experience in my specific product category?**
A knit specialist might struggle with heavy outerwear. Ensure they understand the technical nuances of your particular garments.

**5. How do you handle production delays?**
At what point do they notify you of a delay, and what "Plan B" options (alternative carriers, air-freight subsidies) do they offer?

**6. Can you map your Tier 2 and Tier 3 suppliers?**
Do they know where the fabric and yarn come from, or do they only manage the sewing factory?

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Director's Take**: "The best agents don't just find factories; they find solutions. If an agent can't explain their disaster recovery plan for a port strike, they aren't managing your risk—they're just passing messages."

### The Strategic Relationship

**7. Who is my dedicated account manager?**
You need a single point of contact who understands your brand's voice and quality expectations.

**8. What digital tools do you use for tracking?**
Do they use professional ERP/PLM systems, or is everything managed via loosely organized spreadsheets and emails?

**9. Can I visit the factories unannounced?**
A confident agent with a clean supply chain will always welcome a brand visit to their manufacturing partners.

**10. What are your standard commercial milestones?**
Do you offer flexible timelines, or are there specific project-based milestones? Commercial terms often reflect the agent's operational stability.

#### Vetting FAQ
**Q: Is it better to work with a large agency or a boutique one?**
A: Large agencies have more clout with big factories, but boutique agencies (like Sinha Sourcing) offer significantly more attention and tailored service for growing brands.

**Q: Should the agent handle my shipping too?**
A: Yes. An "End-to-End" agent who handles QC, documentation, and logistics handover ensures there are no gaps in the chain.
