Lead time is the metric that defines the agility of a fashion brand. In an era of algorithm-driven trends and rapid inventory turnover, being late to market can significantly impact the success of a collection. Choosing your sourcing region based on *time* is a critical strategic decision.

### Regional Operational Benchmarks (FOB)

Average timelines from PO placement to "Ready to Ship" depend on material access and technical complexity:

-   **Bangladesh**: Highly efficient for vertical knit production and large-scale woven cycles. Significant investments in infrastructure are continuously improving the speed of these hubs.
-   **Vietnam**: Excels in agility for complex outerwear and technical collections, often benefiting from proximity to specialized component hubs.
-   **India**: Offers specialized speed for fashion-forward items and artisan-influenced categories with a growing vertically integrated material base.

### Strategic Regional Advantages

1.  **Component Proximity**: Regions with closer proximity to specialized fabric and trim hubs can often shave days off the development cycle, particularly for technically complex items.
2.  **Vertical Integration**: Hubs that spin, knit, and dye fabric on the same campus as the assembly (Composite Mills) allow for significantly shorter timelines for core products.
3.  **Trade Dynamics**: Strategic regional trade status can simplify customs clearance processes for specific destination markets, contributing to a smoother total time-to-market.

> [!NOTE]
> **Director's Take**: "The real lead time is the 'Total Time to Market.' A region might have a slightly longer manufacturing cycle but provide logistics or trade advantages that result in faster final delivery to the warehouse."

### Factors That Influence Timelines

-   **Component Availability**: Local sourcing of trims and accessories is the primary way to reduce the risk of delay.
-   **Approval Velocity**: The speed of brand-side decisions—from lab-dip approvals to sample sign-offs—is often the largest variable in the critical path.
-   **Calendar Planning**: Strategic planning around regional holidays and peak capacity seasons ensures that production slots remain protected.
