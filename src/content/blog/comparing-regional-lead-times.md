Lead time is the metric that keeps fashion directors awake at night. In an era of algorithm-driven trends and rapid inventory turnover, being 15 days late can reduce the value of a collection by 40%. Choosing your sourcing region based on *time* is now just as critical as choosing it based on *cost*.

### The Standard Industry Benchmarks (FOB)

Average timelines from PO placement to "Ready to Ship" (Ex-Factory):

| Region | Basic Apparel | Complex Outerwear | Fashion-Forward (Knit) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bangladesh** | 60–75 Days | 90+ Days | 45–60 Days |
| **Vietnam** | 50–65 Days | 75–85 Days | 40–50 Days |
| **India** | 55–70 Days | 80+ Days | 40–55 Days |

### Why Vietnam is Often Faster
Vietnam’s proximity to China allows for ultra-fast "fabric-in-transit" times. For items requiring specialized technical fabrics or trims, Vietnamese factories can often shave 10 days off the cycle simply due to geographical convenience.

### The Bangladesh "Speed Revolution"
While historically slower due to port congestion, Bangladesh is catching up. The growth of **Composite Mills**—which spin, knit, and dye fabric on the same campus as they sew—allows for "Vertical Integration" that can achieve lead times under 45 days for core jersey products (T-shirts, hoodies).

> [!NOTE]
> **Director's Take**: "The real lead time is the 'Total Time to Market.' Bangladesh might take 10 days longer to sew, but its duty-free status (EBA) saves 3-5 days in customs clearance compared to non-EU-FTA regions."

### 3 Factors That Can Crash Your Timeline
1.  **Trim Delays**: A missing zipper or a late-delivered button can halt 5,000 garments. Always source trims locally when possible.
2.  **Communication Gaps**: Every 24 hours you wait to approve a lab-dip or a sample is an extra day on the end of the production cycle.
3.  **National Holidays**: Never schedule a critical launch around Lunar New Year (Vietnam/China) or Eid (Bangladesh) without a 20-day buffer.

### Time-to-Market FAQ
**Q: Can I pay extra for "Express" production?**
A: Yes. Many factories have "Quick Response" lines dedicated to high-margin, small-batch orders with a 30-day turnaround, but expect a 20-30% price surcharge.

**Q: Does air freight save enough time to be worth it?**
A: Sea freight takes 30-40 days to the US/EU; air freight takes 5-7 days. If your retail margin is high and the season is short, air freight is a survival strategy, not an expense.
