While Bangladesh is synonymous with garments, its footwear industry is the "Silent Giant" of the export economy. In 2026, the country has solidified its position as a top-tier destination for leather shoes, sports footwear, and sustainable sandals, driven by a domestic leather supply and a growing technical workforce.

### The Footwear Clusters: Dhaka & Beyond
Footwear production is concentrated in specialized industrial zones around Dhaka and Gazipur. These aren't just converted garment factories; they are purpose-built facilities equipped with Italian and German machinery specifically designed for the complexities of shoe lasting and sole injection.

### Why Bangladesh for Footwear?

-   **Backward Integration (Leather)**: Bangladesh produces some of the highest-quality goat and cow leather in the world. Having the tannery and the shoe factory in the same region significantly reduces material lead times.
-   **GSP Advantage**: Like garments, footwear from Bangladesh enjoys significant duty-free access to the EU and UK markets, providing a 15-20% landed-cost advantage over China.
-   **Technical Diversification**: Beyond leather, factories are now excelling in **Synthetic Footwear** (PU/PVC) and **Athletic Shoes**, catering to the mass-market volume needs of global retailers.

### 3 Tips for Footwear Buyers
1.  **Verify the Lasts**: Ensure the factory has experience with UK/US/EU sizing lasts. A "Size 9" can vary significantly between regions.
2.  **Audit the Chemicals**: If sourcing leather, ensure the tannery is **LWG (Leather Working Group)** certified to meet EU environmental standards.
3.  **Check Sample Accuracy**: Footwear samples are harder to "tweak" than garments. Invest more time in the initial mold and prototype stage.

#### Footwear FAQ
**Q: What is the typical MOQ for leather shoes?**
A: Due to the cost of molds and specialized leather batches, MOQs for footwear are often higher than garments, typically starting at 1,000–2,000 pairs per style.

**Q: Can Bangladesh factories produce waterproof outdoor boots?**
A: Yes. Several top-tier factories now feature Gore-Tex-equivalent membrane technology and seam-sealing capabilities for high-performance outdoor wear.
