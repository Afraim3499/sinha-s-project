Bangladesh is the second-largest apparel exporter in the world, yet many buyers view its industry as a monolith. In reality, the "Bangladesh Ecosystem" is a highly diverse network of specialized factories ranging from 10,000-worker mega-units to agile, tech-focused boutiques.

### Mapping the Factory Landscape

Understanding which category your supplier falls into is the first step toward a successful partnership:

-   **Composite Units**: Massive facilities that handle everything from spinning yarn to final packing. These are ideal for high-volume, low-cost "basics."
-   **Specialized Factories**: Units focused on a single niche—e.g., denim washing, heavy outerwear, or lingerie. These offer superior craftsmanship in their specific category.
-   **Knit vs. Woven Units**: Modern Bangladesh factories are increasingly splitting along these lines to optimize machine layouts and worker specializations.

### The Quality & Compliance Benchmarks
Bangladesh leads the world in green manufacturing. The ecosystem is defined by strict adherence to international standards:
-   **LEED Certification**: More than 270 factories are Lead in Energy and Environmental Design certified.
-   **Accord & Alliance**: Safety frameworks that have fundamentally transformed worker safety in the region.
-   **Better Work Bangladesh**: A partnership between the ILO and the IFC that drives social dialogue and productivity.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Director's Take**: "Don't just look at the machinery; look at the worker welfare programs. A factory with high morale and low turnover will always produce better quality than a high-pressure 'sweatshop' model."

#### Ecosystem FAQ
**Q: Is it better to work with a composite factory?**
A: If your volume is high, yes. It reduces logistical delays between spinning, dyeing, and sewing. However, for specialized items, a niche factory often has better machinery.

**Q: Are all factories in Bangladesh now safe?**
A: The formal export sector has seen a total transformation. By working with a sourcing agent, you ensure you are strictly in the "Formal & Vetted" network.
