The landscape of international apparel trade is undergoing its most significant regulatory shift in a generation. By 2027, the European Union will require every garment sold within its borders to carry a Digital Product Passport (DPP). This isn't just another label; it's a comprehensive digital record of a product's entire lifecycle.

### What is a Digital Product Passport?
A DPP is a machine-readable data carrier (like a QR code or NFC tag) that provides consumers, regulators, and recyclers with essential information about the product's circularity and environmental impact.

**Key Data Points Required:**
- **Product Identification**: SKU, batch number, and place of manufacture.
- **Traceability**: Origin of raw materials (Tier 1-4).
- **Sustainability Credentials**: Carbon footprint, water usage, and certifications (GOTS, Oeko-Tex).
- **Social Compliance**: Labor standards and factory audit scores.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Director's Take**: "Brands that wait until 2026 to start mapping their Tiers 3 and 4 will face massive market access risks. Traceability must begin at the fiber level, today."

### The Bangladesh Readiness Report
Bangladesh is surprisingly well-positioned for this shift. As the global leader in LEED-certified factories, many Tier 1 suppliers already have the digital infrastructure to track energy and waste. Pilot projects are currently underway in Dhaka to integrate blockchain-based traceability into the existing manufacturing ecosystem.

### 5 Steps to Prepare for 2027

1.  **Audit Your Tiers**: Go beyond your direct factory. Identify where your yarn, fabric, and chemicals are coming from.
2.  **Set Data Standards**: Standardize how you collect environmental data across different suppliers.
3.  **Implement Traceability Hardware**: Start testing QR code integration on physical labels.
4.  **Align with Digital Platforms**: Choose a DPP platform that can integrate with your existing PLM (Product Lifecycle Management) system.
5.  **Pilot a Single Category**: Don't wait to do your whole collection. Start with a "Hero" product to iron out the data kinks.

### Digital Passport FAQ
**Q: Does DPP apply to accessories and footwear?**
A: Yes, the mandate covers all textile products sold in the EU, including bags and footwear.

**Q: Who pays for the DPP infrastructure?**
A: While the technology cost is often a brand-level investment, the data collection is a shared responsibility between the brand and the manufacturer.
